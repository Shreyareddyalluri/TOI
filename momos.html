Medu Vada(garelu)

Indian Dish

Method:
Step 1- Soaking the Urad Dal
Take 1 cup of urad dal (black gram, husked)** in a bowl and rinse it thoroughly 2-3 times under running water to remove any impurities. Once cleaned, add enough water to fully submerge the dal and let it soak for at least **4 to 5 hours** to ensure it softens properly. After the soaking time is complete, drain all excess water completely to prepare the dal for grinding.

Step 2- Grinding the Batter
Transfer the soaked urad dal to a wet grinder or high-speed blender. Add very little water (1–2 tablespoons at a time) and grind. Grind until the batter is smooth, thick, and fluffy. Avoid adding too much water, as the batter should hold its shape. Transfer the batter to a large mixing bowl.

Step 3- Preparing the Batter
Add 2 tablespoons of rice flour to the batter for crispiness. Mix in 2 finely chopped green chilies, 1-inch grated ginger, 8–10 chopped curry leaves, 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves, and ½ teaspoon salt. Beat the batter for 2–3 minutes to aerate it, making the vadas light and fluffy. If the batter feels too soft, refrigerate it for 15–20 minutes to firm up.

Step 4- Shaping the Medu Vada and Frying them
Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Wet your hands with water to prevent the batter from sticking. Take a small portion of the batter (about the size of a lemon). Flatten it slightly and poke a hole in the center with your finger to create the doughnut shape. Alternatively, shape the vadas using a banana leaf or plastic sheet. Check if the oil is hot by dropping a tiny portion of batter—it should sizzle and rise immediately. Carefully slide the shaped vadas into the hot oil. Fry in small batches, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan. Flip occasionally using a slotted spoon to fry evenly. Fry until the vadas turn golden brown and crispy on both sides. Remove from oil and place on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
