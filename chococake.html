Double ka Meetha

Dessert

Total Calories: 352 calories
Ingredients:
  • 4 slices of white bread (preferably a day or two old)
    		•
  • 2 cups full-fat milk
    		•
  • ½ cup sugar (adjust to taste)
    		•
  • 1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter)
    		•
  • 1/4 cup cashews
    		•
  • 1/4 cup almonds (slivered)
    		•
  • 1/4 cup pistachios (slivered)
    		•
  • 1/4 cup golden raisins
    		•
  • 1 teaspoon cardamom powder
    		•
  • A few strands of saffron (optional)
    		•
  • Rose water or kewra water (optional, for fragrance)
    		•
Method:
Step 1: Prepare the Bread
Trim the edges of the bread slices to make them neat. Cut the bread into halves or quarters to make triangles or rectangles. Heat 2 tablespoons of ghee in a pan and fry the bread slices until they are golden and crisp on both sides. Be sure not to over-fry them, as they should remain soft on the inside.

Step 2: Make the Sugar Syrup
In another pan, add 1/2 cup of sugar and 1/2 cup of water. Heat it on medium flame until the sugar dissolves completely. Once the sugar syrup is ready, add a few saffron strands (optional), cardamom powder, and a few drops of rose water or kewra water for extra fragrance (optional). Let it simmer for 2–3 minutes. Once done, remove from heat.

Step 3: Prepare the Milk Mixture
In a separate saucepan, boil 2 cups of milk on medium flame. Once it starts boiling, reduce the heat to low and let the milk simmer for 5–7 minutes, allowing it to thicken slightly. You can add a bit more sugar to the milk if you prefer it sweeter. If using, you can add a pinch of saffron strands to the milk for color and aroma. Stir well to dissolve.

Step 4: Fry the Dry Fruits
In the same pan where you fried the bread, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee. Fry cashews, almonds, pistachios, and raisins until golden brown. Be careful not to burn them. Remove the dry fruits from the ghee and set aside.

Step 5: Assemble the Double Ka Meetha
In a serving dish, arrange the fried bread slices in a layer. Pour the sugar syrup evenly over the bread slices. Let it soak for a few minutes until the bread absorbs the syrup. Then, pour the thickened milk mixture over the bread slices, ensuring it’s well-covered. Garnish with the fried dry fruits on top.

Step 6: Let It Soak and Serve
Let the Double Ka Meetha sit for about 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together and the dessert to absorb the milk. Serve it warm or chilled, depending on your preference.
